Looks pretty good, right? Well, I was happy. {{ macros.render_figure('', "See if you can spot all of the isolated ground planes.")}} I had another board made by the good men and women of AP Circuits. I happily toasted it up. {{ macros.render_figure('', "That's 246 ℃ ")}} The finished board, bristling with muxes and PGAs and all the rest. {{ macros.render_figure('', "")}} Now, some of the savvier among you may note that there are substantial errors in this circuit. To those of you, I care not, for I forge ahead like a train in the night. So, at some point in the development process, I did something bad. And hardware ain't like software. Whatever you do gets baked in (I guess software used to be like that--no longer in our web-ish days). After pouring the ground, so as to free some visual space for laying down components--I hid it--along with the airwires{{ macros.renderfootnote("footnote-fail-1", "1")}} that it came with. Naturally, I then promptly forgot I did any such thing, and proceeded my merry way, getting the board made and all the rest. {{ macros.render_figure('', "")}} {{ macros.render_figure('', "")}}
